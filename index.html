FAQ

What's a CTF?

A CTF or Capture the Flag, in terms of cybersecurity/info security, is a competition where participants attempt to obtain a flag (more on that later). There are many different formats of CTF, but we primarily compete in Jeopardy-style CTFs. Like Jeopardy, a CTF of this style requires knowledge of various different skills. For example, a Jeopardy-style CTF will have various challenges in categories, such as but not limited to: Web, Forensics, Crytography, Reverse Engineering, and Stegonography. Examples of these kinds of CTF are CSAW and EasyCTF.

What's a flag, and how do you find one?

In terms of a CTF competition, a flag is a string of text that is used to solve the problem. The flag is usually hidden within a file, series of websites, a disk image, encrypted/encoded, or even within an image. In most competitions, a flag will look like this: 

flag{this_is_a_flag}
However, the format of a flag may vary depending on the competition or the challenge writer (i.e. the writer might specify that the flag for a specific challenge does not follow the conventional flag format).

So, what's this then?

This site serves as a way to help prepare you for a CTF, as the name of the site implies. We have collected and compiled various CTF problems; some of them are from past CTFs, and some have been created for this CTF. To emulate a CTF, the challenges range in difficulty and skill set, and are presented in a format typical of many CTFs.
